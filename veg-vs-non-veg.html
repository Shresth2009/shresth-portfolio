Veg vs Non-Veg: What’s Actually Better?

Posted by: Shresth | Date: June 17, 2025

About two months ago, something changed in our kitchen. My mother decided to stop cooking non-veg food due to religious reasons. So, my dad, brother, and I also shifted to a vegetarian diet. This made me curious — is this actually a better diet? Or were we missing something important?

I watched a detailed video by Gaurav Thakur on this topic and did some of my own research. I found out that this debate isn't about what's "right" or "wrong" — it’s about balance, health, and awareness.

🥦 Benefits of a Veg/Vegan Diet:

  • Rich in fiber, antioxidants, and natural nutrients.
  • Helps in reducing cholesterol and preventing heart disease.
  • Environmentally sustainable — less water and land usage.
  • Many top athletes have switched to plant-based diets.

🍗 Benefits of a Non-Veg Diet:

  • Great source of complete proteins like chicken, eggs, and fish.
  • Provides Vitamin B12, iron, and Omega-3 — essential for brain and body.
  • Helps in muscle growth and strength development.

⚖️ My Conclusion:

Both diets have their pros and cons. If planned properly, a vegetarian diet can meet all nutritional needs. And if eaten in moderation, non-veg can be powerful too. So the real answer isn’t "Veg or Non-Veg", it’s “How educated are you about your food?”

For me, this change taught a lot. Not just about health — but also about research, belief, and learning with curiosity. That’s what makes science and life both interesting!
